How to build Photoshop - WebGL not just for 3D
Web EN 45 min

WebGL is normally associated with 3D graphics, which can seem daunting without a background in this field. However, the WebGL API is inherently 2D, merely supplying a very useful toolbox of functionality for creating 3D engines. In this talk, we'll explore how WebGL can be used to manipulate 2D graphics, by creating a simple image editing application. Through live-coding we'll see how setting up a WebGL application isn't all that complicated and can be achieved without having to reach for higher level frameworks, and how hardware accelerated image effects like color changes, deformations or blurs can be implemented in a few lines of code.

  • Felix Palmer

    Coming from a physics background, Felix got into software by writing games in Flash. Since then he has spent time working in London and Silicon Valley, working with a wide range of technologies, from mobile to cloud, server to web. He enjoys combining the visual with the technical and is excited about the opportunities WebGL brings.

    Currently he lives in Prague, spending his time building things with WebGL, speaking at conferences, and writing on www.pheelicks.com.

Bringing tech innovation to Elephants
Business EN 45 min

Large companies tend to have a very difficult relationship with technology, many of them spending in a bad manner their large budgets and almost all of them being allergic to innovative technologies, even if no one will openly admit it.

If you plan to work on non-traditional IT stuff in a large company, or if you want to approach one of them with a break-through technical solution, or if are just curious about how are things working there, then I hope that some of my personal experiences in working with many “Elephants” will help you to figure it out what the so-called “Top Managers” and their organizations really understand and think of all the new technologies stuff you are likely very passionate about.

  • Enrico Scopa

    Enrico Scopa, born in July 1968 in Italy, since 2007 lives in Czech Republic with his family (one wife only and 3 young daughters, 2 of them born in Prague). Passionate about technology since ever, he graduated in Electronic Engineering in Bologna and then spent 5 years at Andersen Consulting (now Accenture) mostly on custom system development and system integration work, and 15 years at McKinsey & co, becoming the Partner responsible for the Business Technology Office in Central Europe. During his career at McKinsey, he worked on Technology Management topics with over 40 companies located all over Western and Central Eastern Europe, Russia, Ukraine and South-Africa. He is now co-founder of ENRIAN Partners, a recently born company based on an innovative and rules-breaking model of design and delivery technology solutions to corporations. He loves sci fi, skiing, scuba diving, sailing, basketball, beach tennis and pancaking for the family with his Iron Man costume.
